Customer Info Page Redesign

My role

Working on my own, understanding the needs of CS agents, coming up with a redesign proposal and validate it

Problem definition

Customer support agents use the customer info page to solve CS tickets. However, it is hard to use because: 

  • Information is all over the place and a lot of brain power is required to remember routes to find necessary information.
  • The visual design is not clean and clear. Information is crowded.

    • Success metrics

  • Qualitative: CS agents feel easier and less stressed to solve CS tickets.
  • Quantitative: The average time for solving a CS ticket is reduced.

    • Understand the pain - interviews

    To have a deep understanding of the problem before getting to a solution, I interviewed 5 CS agents and found an answer to the question "why it is hard to use". Usually, agents need to go to an order info site but it is hard to find a link. Or if they want to look at a customer financial history, this info is behind 8 clicks. And important info such as customer log-in access stays after 4 clicks and lack visual emphasis. Bringing commonly searched information out of the deep ground is the key to solve some pain.

    Co-create with CS agents

    After interviews, I organized a card sorting workshop with CS agents to create a new information architecture. They are the experts to sort out info that is looked up frequently. And they also taught me what info needs to be found together for solving a type of CS ticket. I made the final information architecture map after the workshop.

    Visual design - clean, clear and space to breath

    Solving CS tickets is a stressful and fast-paced task, I addressed 3 keywords for the visuals: clean, clear and space to breath. As information can be overloaded to the CS agents, more space between information and clear visual hierarchies will make their life easier.

    Prototype and qualitative validation

    In order to test the design before implementation, a high-fidelity prototype is made to gather user feedback. As expected, although there were some small change requests, CS agents were very positive about the new design and had a confidence score 9/10 that it will increase their efficiency.

    The next step 

    After the implementation, an A/B test will be conducted to test if the new design reduces the average time spent in solving a CS ticket. Moreover, Hotjar will be used to track if there is a page that agents bounce very quickly. If so, I need to find out what they are missing on that page.

    Lessons learned

    Work close with users: Interview with the agents and the workshop really helped me put myself into users' shoes and understand the problems. It made the design process fast and also gave me confidence in designing.

    Just start somewhere: This project sounds big at the beginning and I wanted to solve everything in one goal. But thinking in this way does not help. Instead, start somewhere and solve the most painful problems first. And later on, address other problems gradually.
